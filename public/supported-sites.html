Strivve Supported Sites: A Living Ecosystem

How Strivve monitors, tests, and prioritizes our broad portfolio of merchant sites to deliver reliable card placement.

The Big Idea

Every merchant site Strivve supports is a living, dynamic entity. Sites can and do go up and down at any time due to merchant-side changes — UI redesigns, security updates, fraud detection changes, rate limiting, and platform migrations. This is normal. What matters is how quickly we detect it, how we respond, and how we prioritize.

Strivve is always aware of site status changes. We prioritize resolution starting with our highest-impact sites, informed by customer feedback, cross-customer popularity, and payments industry research.

How We Stay on Top of It

Bi-Weekly Automated Testing

Supported sites are tested on a rigorous bi-weekly cycle to verify end-to-end card placement for CardUpdatr and CardSavr.

Production Telemetry

Every cardholder interaction is instrumented. Real production usage gives us more coverage than any manual testing could — a technology advantage built over years.

Watchlist Protocol

Any site failure triggers immediate daily monitoring for a week. We decide to temporarily pull the site or keep it active based on real data — never guesswork.

How We Prioritize

With a broad and growing portfolio of supported sites, not every issue is addressed at once. Our prioritization framework:

  • Customer Feedback — Your reports directly influence what gets fixed first. We want to hear from you.
  • Cross-Customer Popularity — The most-used sites across all Strivve partners are prioritized first.
  • Payments Research — We proactively track industry trends to add and prioritize emerging merchant sites.
  • Campaign Support — Planning a promotion that features specific merchant sites? Let us know at helpdesk@strivve.com and we’ll put extra eyes on those sites to ensure they’re performing at their best throughout your campaign.

What This Means for You

If you see a site listed as “Limited” or “In Maintenance,” it does not mean cardholders are stuck. Strivve is already aware and actively working on it. A temporarily unavailable site is not an abandoned site — our team works every day to correct, improve, and restore sites. The same site may be fully operational tomorrow, and a different site may rotate out briefly next week. That is the nature of a living ecosystem, and Strivve is built to manage it.

Your cardholders will still see the full list of available merchants and can update any active site in the meantime. Site availability fluctuations do not affect the overall CardUpdatr experience — cardholders simply choose from whichever sites are live at that moment.
